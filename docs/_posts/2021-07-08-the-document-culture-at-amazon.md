According to Senior Amazon Dev Justin Garrison[^1], the interesting part about
the 6-pager culture isn't the format of the document, but how it is used.
Meetings start with reading, and how long it lasts depends on the length of the
document. The meeting must be scheduled for enough time to read and discuss.

It doesn't work to read the document before hand, because you'll inevitably
face one of three outcomes:
* No one read it and everything still needs to be explained in the meeting
* Some read it but forgot what its about because it was read days before
* At least one person had a question that could be answered via email


Some benefits to putting it into practice are:
* Presenters don't have to feel nervous about presenting in front of people
* Documents help eliminate many biases, for or against, the person who wrote
the document
* You read everything in your own voice and vocal communication are not present
in the document
* There's no "can you see my screen" stuff
* There's a wealth of current and past documents. You can go back and look at them.
* You are not expected to retain document information outside of the meeting. Feedback and discussions happen during the meeting.
* If a document is provided early and I have a conflict I can read the document ahead of time and join the meeting 10-20 minutes late without missing anything.
* Document reading from home can as time for exercise
